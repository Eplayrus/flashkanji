Hiragana and Katakana

Hiragana

  • に · ni — direction/time particle
  • へ · e — movement direction
  • を · o — object marker

Katakana

  • アプリ · apuri — app
  • メール · meeru — mail
  • カレンダー · karendaa — calendar

Anchor Words

  • 日本 · nihon — Japan. language and country selectors
  • 学校 · gakkou — school. maps, profiles, schedules
  • 月曜日 · getsuyoubi — Monday. calendar and reminders

Blank Sentence Drills

  • 私は___へ行きます。
    わたしは がっこうへ いきます。
    I go to school.
  • ___語を読みます。
    にほんごを よみます。
    I read Japanese.

Textbook Goals

  • Recognize basic kanji in menus, dates, and short notifications.
  • Separate onyomi and kunyomi instead of merging every reading together.
  • Understand simple phrases with は, を, に, へ, and です.

What To Learn First

N5 relies on high-frequency characters such as 日, 本, 人, 月, 火, 水, 木, 金, 土, 学, 校, 先, 生. They appear in dates, profiles, settings, and schedules.

  • Start with the meaning and one core example word.
  • For interfaces, words like 日時, 名前, 学校, 先生, 入口 matter early.

Readings

Onyomi often appears in compounds: 学校 = がっこう. Kunyomi often appears with hiragana endings: 行く = いく.

  • Do not memorize every reading at once. Attach readings to words.
  • If kana follows the kanji, think of kunyomi first.
